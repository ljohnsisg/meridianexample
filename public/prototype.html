Resume tips for a first flying job

  • 1Lead with your numbers. Put total, PIC, cross-country, and instrument time in a stats line at the top — recruiters scan for minimums first.
  • 2One page, reverse chronological. Certificates and ratings up top, then experience. Skip the objective statement.
  • 3Quantify everything. "Flew 40+ cross-country hours into Class B" beats "experienced in busy airspace."
  • 4Match the role's language. A survey job wants steady low-level VFR; a 135 job wants IFR currency. Mirror their words.
  • 5List currency & medical. First-class medical date, flight review, IPC — recruiters look for these explicitly.
